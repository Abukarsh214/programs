What is an ADN Degree in Nursing

Registered nursing is one of the most rewarding career options available.
In fact, recent research shows that almost 95 percent of Registered Nurses
(RNs) today are happy in their chosen career path. Most nurses feel gratified
by helping others in need, and having the ability to make a difference
in their patients’ lives.

Others are proud of their career, and enjoy the
flexibility of the job. If you are considering a nursing career, you can
look forward to many benefits – both personally and professionally.
The question is, where do you begin?

Degree Requirement

An associate degree in Nursing (ADN) is the standard pathway taken to become a Registered Nurse. An ADN is also the minimum degree required to become a licensed RN. Those who complete an associate degree in Nursing are eligible to take the NCLEX-RN, a board examination that grants licensure to RNs in the United States. An RN must be licensed in order to practice in the nursing field
